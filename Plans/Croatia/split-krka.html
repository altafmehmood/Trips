Day 7 - Thursday, April 30
Split - Diocletian's Palace

üèõÔ∏è Diocletian's Palace (UNESCO)

  • Built AD 305 by Roman Emperor Diocletian as retirement palace
  • 1,700 years old and remarkably well-preserved
  • Not just a palace - entire living quarter of Split (3,000 residents live inside!)
  • Covers 30,000 square meters - half the Old Town
  • UNESCO World Heritage Site since 1979
  • One of best-preserved Roman monuments in the world

‚õ™ Cathedral of St. Domnius

  • World's oldest Catholic cathedral still in use in its original structure
  • Originally Diocletian's mausoleum (AD 305)
  • Converted to cathedral in 7th century - ultimate irony (Diocletian persecuted Christians)
  • Climb the bell tower - 60m high, panoramic views over Split and islands
  • Narrow spiral staircase but worth every step

üèõÔ∏è Palace Highlights

  • Peristyle - Central square, once palace's ceremonial court
  • Basement Halls - Underground palace chambers (Game of Thrones filming location)
  • Golden Gate - Northern entrance with Gregory of Nin statue (rub big toe for luck!)
  • Silver Gate, Bronze Gate, Iron Gate - Other historical palace entrances
  • Vestibule - Domed entrance hall, famous for acoustics

üèñÔ∏è Riva Promenade

  • Palm-lined waterfront promenade along harbor
  • Perfect for people-watching with coffee
  • Lined with cafes and restaurants
  • Views of ferries departing to islands
  • Lively atmosphere day and night

üèñÔ∏è Baƒçvice Beach

Day 8 - Friday, May 1
Krka National Park & Drive South

üöó Drive to Krka National Park

  • 1 hour from Split (87km)
  • Named after Krka River - 72km long with seven waterfalls
  • Different character from Plitvice - more Mediterranean vegetation

üí¶ Skradinski Buk Waterfall

  • Croatia's most photographed waterfall
  • 17 cascading waterfalls over 800m length
  • 45.7 meters total height drop
  • Emerald green pools and travertine formations
  • Small island in middle with traditional watermills
  • Swimming allowed in designated area below falls (if weather permits)

ü•æ Walking Trails

  • Well-maintained wooden boardwalks around falls
  • 2-3 hours to explore main area at leisurely pace
  • Multiple viewpoints of cascades from different angles
  • Walk to old water mills - some still functioning
  • Ethnographic exhibition in restored stone houses

üå≥ What Makes Krka Special

  • More accessible than Plitvice - easier walking
  • Swimming allowed (unlike Plitvice)
  • Rich biodiversity - 860 plant species
  • Cultural heritage - watermills, monasteries
  • April: High water flow, lush greenery, fewer crowds

üí° Split & Krka Tips
Local Insights

Split Tips

  • Palace is free to wander - no entrance fee for streets
  • Pay only for cathedral/bell tower and basement halls
  • Early morning (before 9 AM) best for photos without crowds
  • Palace gets very crowded with cruise ship passengers midday
  • Explore side streets - best restaurants hidden away

Krka Tips

  • Entrance at Lozovac - most popular and closest to Skradinski Buk
  • Shuttle bus from parking to falls (included in ticket)
  • Bring swimsuit if weather warm - swimming area wonderful
  • Wear water-resistant shoes - boardwalks get wet from spray
  • Book tickets online to skip lines

Photography

  • Split: Golden hour at Riva promenade magical
  • Krka: Morning light best for waterfall photos
  • Wide angle lens useful for both palace courtyards and waterfalls

Time Management

  • Split Palace: 3-4 hours minimum to see highlights
  • Krka National Park: 2-3 hours for Skradinski Buk area
  • Driving Split to Dubrovnik: 3-3.5 hours without stops
  • Plan accordingly based on where staying night 7
