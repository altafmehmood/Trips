Flying Across the Pacific
The Journey to the Land Down Under

International Flights

Our Australian adventure began with United Airlines flight UA101 from Houston to Sydney - a massive 8,999-mile journey across the Pacific. At 17 hours and 51 minutes, this was the longest flight either of us had ever taken. Departing Houston at 8:29 PM on December 4th, we wouldn't touch down in Sydney until 7:20 AM on December 6th - crossing the International Date Line and skipping December 5th entirely.

The Boeing 787-9 Dreamliner proved to be an excellent choice for this ultra-long-haul flight. United's service was exceptional - the food was surprisingly good for airline fare, and the crew was attentive throughout the journey. Most importantly, we managed to sleep for over 8 hours on the flight, which made all the difference in fighting jet lag when we arrived.

Before boarding, we relaxed in the AMEX Centurion Lounge at Houston IAH, which offered a quiet refuge and quality refreshments before the long journey ahead. Security at IAH was remarkably efficient, getting us through in no time.

The return journey on UA100 was even slightly shorter at 15 hours 39 minutes, flying 9,114 miles back to Houston on the same 787-9 Dreamliner. Departing Sydney at 11:46 AM, we arrived in Houston at 10:25 AM the same day - the magic of time zones working in our favor. Both United flights arrived on time, bookending our trip perfectly.

Flight Stats: The IAH-SYD route covered 8,999 miles - roughly the distance from New York to Bangkok, making it one of the longest non-stop flights in the world.

Domestic Flights

Our internal Australian flights on Qantas were excellent. All four domestic segments operated smoothly and offered complimentary snacks and drinks - a pleasant surprise compared to U.S. domestic carriers.

Sydney to Melbourne (QF419): Our first domestic leg on a Boeing 737-800 was smooth and efficient. The 90-minute flight gave us a quick transition from harbour city to Victoria's capital.

Melbourne to Hobart (QF1293): The modern Airbus A220-200 was a highlight - this relatively new aircraft is spacious, quiet, and features large windows. The 54-minute hop over Bass Strait offered stunning aerial views of Tasmania's rugged coastline.

Hobart to Sydney (QF996): Our final domestic segment back to Sydney on another Boeing 737-800 arrived 13 minutes early, giving us extra time to enjoy our final evening in the harbour city.

Airport security in Sydney, Melbourne, and Hobart was refreshingly efficient. No long lines, professional staff, and smooth processes made our connections stress-free. In Sydney, we enjoyed the Priority Pass lounge before our domestic flights, which provided a comfortable space to relax and grab a bite.

Day 1-2: Sydney
December 6-7 ‚Ä¢ Harbour City & Coastal Walks

After an overnight flight from Houston, we landed in Sydney to perfect summer weather. The Shangri-La Sydney offered stunning views across the harbour, with the iconic Sydney Harbour Bridge right in our sightline.

Our first day included the exhilarating BridgeClimb experience, offering panoramic 360-degree views of the harbour, Opera House, and the sprawling city below. The climb was worth every step for those breathtaking vistas.

Day two took us on the famous Bondi to Coogee coastal walk - a 6km clifftop journey passing pristine beaches with turquoise waters lapping against golden sands. Each cove revealed a new perspective of Sydney's stunning coastline.

BridgeClimb Sydney: Scaling the iconic Sydney Harbour Bridge arch to 134 meters above sea level - one of the most thrilling experiences in Sydney with unmatched 360¬∞ views of the harbour, Opera House, and cityscape.

Day 3-4: Great Ocean Road
December 8-9 ‚Ä¢ Australia's Coastal Masterpiece

After a smooth 90-minute Qantas flight from Sydney to Melbourne (QF419), complete with complimentary snacks and drinks, we picked up our rental car at Melbourne Airport and immediately headed south. Our first stop was the scenic Millionaire Walk, a beautiful coastal path that gave us our first taste of Victoria's stunning shoreline.

The next day, we embarked on the iconic Great Ocean Road drive. The Twelve Apostles and Loch Ard Gorge were simply breathtaking - massive limestone stacks rising from the Southern Ocean, sculpted by millions of years of wind and waves. The hidden beach at Loch Ard Gorge, accessible through a natural archway in the cliffs, was a highlight with its turquoise waters and golden sand.

We continued through charming coastal towns like Lorne and Aireys Inlet, each offering their own slice of coastal paradise. The Split Point Lighthouse at Aireys Inlet stood sentinel over dramatic clifftops, while the beaches at Anglesea and Lorne beckoned with their pristine beauty.

Must-See: Loch Ard Gorge's secluded beach - a stunning natural amphitheater carved into the limestone cliffs with crystal-clear water.

Day 5-6: Hobart to Strahan
December 10-11 ‚Ä¢ Waterfalls, Rainforests & Gordon River

Flying into Tasmania from Melbourne on Qantas flight QF1293 felt like entering another world. The quick 54-minute flight on the modern Airbus A220-200 offered stunning aerial views as we descended into Hobart. Security at Melbourne Airport was efficient as always, and we landed in Hobart ready for adventure. We immediately drove to Mount Wellington for our first taste of Tasmania's wilderness - the views from the summit were spectacular.

The drive from Hobart to Strahan took us through Mount Field National Park, where we encountered our first platypus in the wild! This shy creature was foraging in a crystal-clear stream, occasionally surfacing for air. Russell Falls thundered nearby, cascading through ancient rainforest.

Strahan, a quaint fishing village on Macquarie Harbour, served as our base for the Gordon River Cruise. The half-day journey took us through pristine wilderness, past ancient Huon pines over 2,000 years old, and to Sarah Island's haunting convict settlement. The seabirds gliding alongside our boat and the rugged lighthouse standing guard at the harbour entrance made for magical moments.

Day 7: Cradle Mountain
December 12-13 ‚Ä¢ Ancient Peaks & Alpine Lakes

Cradle Mountain lived up to its reputation as one of Tasmania's crown jewels. The iconic jagged peak reflected perfectly in Dove Lake, creating one of those postcard-perfect scenes that no camera can truly capture.

The Dove Lake Circuit took us through diverse terrain - from beach pebbles along the shoreline to boardwalks through button grass plains, and finally through ancient myrtle forests. Wildlife was abundant: we spotted wombats, wallabies, and countless bird species.

Early morning at Ronny Creek rewarded us with close encounters with wombats grazing peacefully, seemingly unbothered by our presence. The alpine landscape, with its dramatic peaks and glacial valleys, felt untouched and timeless.

Don't Miss: The Dove Lake Circuit at sunrise when the mountain's reflection is crystal clear and wildlife is most active.

Day 8-9: Freycinet Peninsula
December 13-14 ‚Ä¢ Wineglass Bay & Coastal Paradise

Freycinet National Park saved the best for last. The drive to Coles Bay offered glimpses of the stunning coastline to come, with the pink granite peaks of the Hazards mountains dominating the landscape.

The hike to Wineglass Bay Lookout is deservedly famous. After a steady climb through native forest, the viewpoint revealed one of the world's most beautiful beaches - a perfect crescent of white sand embraced by turquoise waters and framed by pink granite mountains. The color of the water seemed almost unreal in its intensity.

We descended to the beach itself early the next morning, having it almost entirely to ourselves. The silence was broken only by gentle waves and bird calls. Walking along Wineglass Bay's pristine shores, with the Hazards towering above and crystal-clear water lapping at our feet, felt like stepping into paradise.

The coastal scenery around Coles Bay offered endless photo opportunities - from serene sunset views over the bay to the dramatic blue waters contrasting with the orange-red granite rocks.

Tasmania's Treasure: Wineglass Bay truly deserves its reputation as one of the world's top 10 beaches. The combination of the perfect curve, pristine white sand, and impossibly blue water is unforgettable.

Day 10-11: Return to Sydney
December 14-16 ‚Ä¢ An Unexpected Bonus Day

After an early departure from Freycinet, we drove back to Hobart for our flight to Sydney. The Qantas QF996 flight departed Hobart at 4:53 PM and arrived in Sydney at 6:32 PM - actually 13 minutes early. Security at Hobart Airport was the smoothest of the trip, and the Boeing 737-800 was comfortable for the 100-minute journey.

We checked into the Kimpton Margot Sydney for our final evening. The Sydney Opera House and Harbour Bridge, illuminated against the night sky, provided a perfect bookend to our Australian adventure. Walking through Circular Quay at sunset, watching the ferries crisscross the harbour and the city lights begin to twinkle, we couldn't help but feel grateful for this incredible journey.

Flight Cancellation: Our United flight UA100 scheduled for December 15th was cancelled when the incoming aircraft from Houston went technical and had to turn back mid-flight. We were rescheduled to depart on December 16th, giving us an unexpected bonus day in Sydney!

Bonus Day - December 15th: What could have been a frustrating travel disruption turned into a delightful extra day to explore Sydney. We checked into the Sofitel Sydney Darling Harbour, enjoying the hotel's modern luxury and waterfront location. From Darling Harbour, we caught a ferry to Circular Quay, then continued on to Taronga Zoo, perched on the harbour's northern shore with spectacular views back to the city skyline. The ferry ride itself was a highlight, offering stunning perspectives of the Opera House and Harbour Bridge from the water. The zoo's collection of Australian wildlife - from koalas and kangaroos to Tasmanian devils - provided one last encounter with the continent's unique fauna.

After the zoo, we took the scenic route back, walking along the harbour foreshore from Circular Quay all the way to Darling Harbour. The waterfront promenade offered constantly changing views of the harbour, passing historic wharves, modern developments, and the iconic Sydney skyline. The walk ended back at Darling Harbour, where we enjoyed the vibrant waterfront precinct. The extra day allowed us to savor Sydney at a more relaxed pace, without the rush of a departure schedule.

Departure Day - December 16th: The next morning, we headed to the airport and relaxed in the Priority Pass lounge before our rescheduled flight. United flight UA100 finally departed at 11:46 AM, and thanks to the time zone magic, we landed in Houston at 10:25 AM the same day - right on schedule after 15 hours and 39 minutes in the air. While the delay added a day to our journey, it gave us unexpected extra time in one of the world's most beautiful cities.

Final Reflections

This Australian adventure exceeded all expectations. From Sydney's iconic harbour to the dramatic coastline of the Great Ocean Road, and finally to Tasmania's pristine wilderness, every day brought new wonders. Even an unexpected flight cancellation turned into a blessing, giving us an extra day to explore Sydney's Taronga Zoo and harbour foreshore.

The wildlife encounters were unforgettable - from the elusive platypus in Mount Field to the friendly wombats at Cradle Mountain, koalas at Taronga Zoo, and abundant birdlife throughout our journey. The landscapes shifted from urban sophistication to rugged coastal beauty to ancient rainforests and alpine peaks, showcasing Australia's incredible natural diversity.

Tasmania, in particular, left a lasting impression. Its combination of world-class hiking, stunning coastlines, unique wildlife, and pristine wilderness areas make it a destination that demands more time than we could give it. Wineglass Bay, Cradle Mountain, and the Gordon River will remain etched in memory.

Australia has a way of getting under your skin. The distances are vast, the landscapes are dramatic, and the experiences are transformative. Sometimes the best travel moments come from the unexpected - our bonus day in Sydney proved that perfectly. We're already planning our return.
