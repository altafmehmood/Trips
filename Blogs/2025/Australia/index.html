Flying Across the Pacific
The Journey to the Land Down Under

International Flights

Our Australian adventure began with United Airlines flight UA101 from Houston to Sydney - a massive 8,999-mile journey across the Pacific. At 17 hours and 51 minutes, this was the longest flight either of us had ever taken. Departing Houston at 8:29 PM on December 4th, we wouldn't touch down in Sydney until 7:20 AM on December 6th - crossing the International Date Line and skipping December 5th entirely.

The Boeing 787-9 Dreamliner proved to be an excellent choice for this ultra-long-haul flight. United's service was exceptional - the food was surprisingly good for airline fare, and the crew was attentive throughout the journey. Most importantly, we managed to sleep for over 8 hours on the flight, which made all the difference in fighting jet lag when we arrived.

Before boarding, we relaxed in the AMEX Centurion Lounge at Houston IAH, which offered a quiet refuge and quality refreshments before the long journey ahead. Security at IAH was remarkably efficient, getting us through in no time.

The return journey on UA100 was even slightly shorter at 15 hours 39 minutes, flying 9,114 miles back to Houston on the same 787-9 Dreamliner. Departing Sydney at 11:46 AM, we arrived in Houston at 10:25 AM the same day - the magic of time zones working in our favor. Both United flights arrived on time, bookending our trip perfectly.

Flight Stats: The IAH-SYD route covered 8,999 miles - roughly the distance from New York to Bangkok, making it one of the longest non-stop flights in the world.

Domestic Flights

Our internal Australian flights on Qantas were excellent. All four domestic segments operated smoothly and offered complimentary snacks and drinks - a pleasant surprise compared to U.S. domestic carriers.

Sydney to Melbourne (QF419): Our first domestic leg on a Boeing 737-800 was smooth and efficient. The 90-minute flight gave us a quick transition from harbour city to Victoria's capital.

Melbourne to Hobart (QF1293): The modern Airbus A220-200 was a highlight - this relatively new aircraft is spacious, quiet, and features large windows. The 54-minute hop over Bass Strait offered stunning aerial views of Tasmania's rugged coastline.

Hobart to Sydney (QF996): Our final domestic segment back to Sydney on another Boeing 737-800 arrived 13 minutes early, giving us extra time to enjoy our final evening in the harbour city.

Airport security in Sydney, Melbourne, and Hobart was refreshingly efficient. No long lines, professional staff, and smooth processes made our connections stress-free. In Sydney, we enjoyed the Priority Pass lounge before our domestic flights, which provided a comfortable space to relax and grab a bite.

Final Reflections

This Australian adventure exceeded all expectations. From Sydney's iconic harbour to the dramatic coastline of the Great Ocean Road, and finally to Tasmania's pristine wilderness, every day brought new wonders. Even an unexpected flight cancellation turned into a blessing, giving us an extra day to explore Sydney's Taronga Zoo and harbour foreshore.

The wildlife encounters were unforgettable - from the elusive platypus in Mount Field to the friendly wombats at Cradle Mountain, koalas at Taronga Zoo, and abundant birdlife throughout our journey. The landscapes shifted from urban sophistication to rugged coastal beauty to ancient rainforests and alpine peaks, showcasing Australia's incredible natural diversity.

Tasmania, in particular, left a lasting impression. Its combination of world-class hiking, stunning coastlines, unique wildlife, and pristine wilderness areas make it a destination that demands more time than we could give it. Wineglass Bay, Cradle Mountain, and the Gordon River will remain etched in memory.

Australia has a way of getting under your skin. The distances are vast, the landscapes are dramatic, and the experiences are transformative. Sometimes the best travel moments come from the unexpected - our bonus day in Sydney proved that perfectly. We're already planning our return.
