Tasmania Adventure
December 10-14 ‚Ä¢ Five Days of Wilderness, Wildlife & Coastal Beauty

Tasmania is Australia's island wilderness - a land of ancient rainforests, glacial mountains, pristine beaches, and unique wildlife found nowhere else on Earth. Over five incredible days, we explored the island's most spectacular national parks and wilderness areas, from the rugged west coast to the stunning Freycinet Peninsula.

Our Tasmania adventure showcased the remarkable diversity of this island state. We hiked through temperate rainforests among trees thousands of years old, stood on clifftops watching the Southern Ocean crash against pink granite shores, spotted wombats grazing in alpine meadows, and gazed upon beaches ranked among the world's most beautiful. Tasmania is a place where nature remains largely untouched, where wilderness areas stretch to the horizon, and where every turn reveals another breathtaking landscape.

This journey took us across the island from east to west and back again, experiencing Tasmania's World Heritage wilderness areas, its iconic mountain landscapes, and its extraordinary coastal scenery. From Hobart to Cradle Mountain, from the Gordon River to Wineglass Bay, every day brought new wonders.

Five Days of Adventure

Day 5: Hobart & Mount Wellington

Our Tasmania adventure began in Hobart, exploring Mount Wellington's summit with panoramic views over the city and southern Tasmania, followed by coastal explorations at Opossum Bay and waterfront dining at Constitution Dock.

Day 6: West Coast Wilderness

The epic drive from Hobart to Strahan took us through Tasmania's World Heritage wilderness - from Russell Falls at Mount Field National Park to the pristine waters of Lake St Clair (Australia's deepest lake), and through the ancient rainforests of Franklin-Gordon Wild Rivers National Park.

Day 7: Gordon River & Strahan

The Gordon River Cruise was a once-in-a-lifetime wilderness experience - cruising through Macquarie Harbour to Heritage Landing where we walked among 2,000-year-old Huon pines. The afternoon brought our journey to Cradle Mountain with evening wildlife spotting and close encounters with wombats.

Day 8: Cradle Mountain

Tasmania's most iconic walk - the Dove Lake Circuit - showcased Cradle Mountain at its finest with perfect morning reflections. We hiked part of the legendary Overland Track to Cradle Lake Lookout, met endangered Tasmanian devils at Devils@Cradle, then faced the challenging 4-hour mountain drive to Freycinet Peninsula.

Day 9: Freycinet Peninsula & Return to Hobart

Our final day exploring Freycinet National Park was spectacular - hiking to Wineglass Bay Lookout (one of the world's top beaches), discovering the pink granite boulders at Sleepy Bay, walking the Cape Tourville lighthouse circuit, and strolling 3km of pristine white sand at Friendly Beaches before our return to Hobart and evening flight to Sydney.

Tasmania Highlights

World Heritage Wilderness: Explored ancient rainforests in Mount Field, Franklin-Gordon and Cradle Mountain, walking among Huon pines thousands of years old.
Iconic Walks: Hiked the Dove Lake Circuit at Cradle Mountain and climbed to Wineglass Bay Lookout, two of Australia's most famous walks.
Unique Wildlife: Close encounters with wombats, wallabies, pademelons, and endangered Tasmanian devils at conservation sanctuaries.
Gordon River Cruise: Journeyed deep into wilderness on Macquarie Harbour, visiting Sarah Island convict settlement and ancient rainforest at Heritage Landing.
Pristine Beaches: Discovered Freycinet's spectacular coastline including Wineglass Bay, pink granite boulders at Sleepy Bay, and 3km of sand at Friendly Beaches.
Glacial Landscapes: Witnessed stunning glacial features at Lake St Clair (Australia's deepest lake) and the dramatic dolerite peaks of Cradle Mountain.

Planning Tips

Allow enough time: Tasmania is larger than it appears on maps. Drives are scenic but winding - factor in extra time for mountain roads and photo stops.
Early starts pay off: Arrive at Cradle Mountain before 7:30am to drive your own vehicle directly to trailheads and avoid crowds. Early morning also offers the best light for photography and wildlife spotting.
Pack for all seasons: Tasmania's weather is famously changeable. Even in summer, bring warm layers, waterproof gear, and be prepared for sudden weather changes in the mountains.
Book accommodations early: Popular lodges like Cradle Mountain Lodge and Freycinet Lodge fill up months in advance, especially during peak season (December-February).
Don't skip Sleepy Bay: Most visitors only see Wineglass Bay, but Sleepy Bay's pink granite boulders and turquoise water are equally spectacular with far fewer people.
Wildlife at dawn and dusk: The best time to spot wombats, wallabies, and other marsupials is early morning or late afternoon around Ronny Creek at Cradle Mountain.
