Flying Across the Atlantic
The Journey to the United Kingdom

Transatlantic Flights

Our United Kingdom adventure began with United Airlines flight UA5 from Houston to London Heathrow - a 5,014-mile journey across the Atlantic. After a gate departure at 7:37 PM on January 9th, we took off at 7:52 PM and touched down at London Heathrow at 10:20 AM on January 10th - an 8 hour 28 minute overnight flight that maximized our time in the UK.

The Boeing 777-200ER proved to be an excellent choice for this transatlantic flight. This twin-engine wide-body aircraft is perfectly suited for long-haul routes, offering comfort and reliability. United's service was excellent, and we managed to get some rest during the overnight flight, arriving refreshed and ready to explore London.

The return journey on flight UA879 was slightly longer at 9 hours 47 minutes, departing London at 10:11 AM and arriving in Houston at 1:58 PM the same day (thanks to favorable time zones). The 5,020-mile westbound route took us back across the Atlantic, completing our British adventure.

Flight Stats: The IAH-LHR route covered over 5,000 miles each way - approximately the distance from New York to London. The Boeing 777-200ER's twin-engine efficiency makes it ideal for these transatlantic operations.

Scenic Drive & Departure

Final Reflections

This United Kingdom adventure showcased the best of Britain's winter beauty. From London's iconic landmarks to the Lake District's dramatic peaks, and from Glasgow's vibrant culture to Edinburgh's historic charm, every day brought new discoveries and experiences.

The Lake District, despite January's challenging weather, revealed itself as one of England's most spectacular regions. Weather-dependent planning added an element of adventure, and the clear summit days rewarded us with views that made every uphill step worthwhile. The combination of waterfalls, mountain tarns, and classic English fells created memories that will last a lifetime.

Scotland exceeded all expectations. The scenic drive from the Lake District through Dumfries and Galloway offered stunning Border country views, while Edinburgh's blend of medieval history and modern sophistication created a perfect finale to our journey. Arthur's Seat provided both a physical challenge and panoramic views across the Scottish capital.

Britain in winter has a unique character - crisp air, dramatic skies, cozy pubs, and landscapes that shift between moody and magnificent by the hour. The blend of urban exploration and mountain hiking, of Michelin dining and casual fish and chips, of ancient history and modern culture - it all combined to create an unforgettable journey. We'll be back.
