Day 1: London

Our United Kingdom adventure began with an overnight flight from Houston, landing at London Heathrow at 10:15 AM on January 10th. After a smooth arrival and efficient passport control, we took the Piccadilly Line from Hatton Cross station into central London, getting off at Knightsbridge - one of the easiest and most economical airport-to-city connections in Europe.

Despite jet lag from the overnight transatlantic flight, we were eager to explore London on foot. Our day-long walking tour would take us through some of the city's most iconic landmarks, covering several miles of central London's most historic neighborhoods.

Tube Travel: We took the Piccadilly Line from Hatton Cross (near Heathrow) to Knightsbridge to start our walking tour, and returned via Green Park station to Hatton Cross after dinner. The Piccadilly Line offers direct access from Heathrow to central London. Simply tap contactless payment at the gates - fares range from ¬£1.80 to ¬£5.80 depending on zones and time of day.

Knightsbridge to Buckingham Palace

Emerging from Knightsbridge tube station, our first stop was Harrods, the world-famous luxury department store. The iconic terracotta building with its elaborate facade and window displays has been a Knightsbridge landmark since 1849. Even from the outside, the grandeur and scale of this seven-story retail palace impressed.

From Harrods, we walked southwest toward Buckingham Palace, passing through the elegant streets of Belgravia. Arriving at Buckingham Palace, the London residence and administrative headquarters of the British monarch, we admired the imposing neoclassical facade and the Victoria Memorial that stands prominently in front. While the Changing of the Guard wasn't scheduled for our arrival day, the palace's grandeur and the crowds of tourists photographing the famous gates created a lively atmosphere.

Westminster & Whitehall

From Buckingham Palace, we walked east through St James's Park toward Westminster, passing through one of London's eight Royal Parks. The park's lake, with its resident pelicans and waterfowl, offered peaceful views despite the January chill.

Arriving at Westminster, we stood before the Gothic Revival magnificence of the Palace of Westminster and its famous clock tower - Big Ben. The intricate stonework and towering spires create one of the most recognizable silhouettes in the world. We visited Westminster Abbey, the coronation church where British monarchs have been crowned since 1066. This Gothic masterpiece, with its soaring vaulted ceilings and intricate fan vaulting, stands as a testament to over 1,000 years of English history.

Next, we made our way to the London Eye, the massive observation wheel on the South Bank of the Thames. From there, we walked through Whitehall to see 10 Downing Street, the official residence and office of the Prime Minister. The famous black door and security gates have been the center of British political power for nearly 300 years, though we could only view it from behind the security barriers at the end of Downing Street.

Trafalgar Square to the City

We continued to Trafalgar Square, one of London's most famous public spaces, with its towering Nelson's Column and the National Gallery backdrop. The square was alive with street performers and tourists despite the January weather.

Our walking route then took us eastward across the Millennium Bridge, the sleek pedestrian suspension bridge that connects the South Bank with the City of London. This modern structure, opened in 2000, offered dramatic views of St. Paul's Cathedral ahead and the Thames below. We passed St. Paul's Cathedral, Sir Christopher Wren's baroque masterpiece crowned by one of the world's most recognizable domes, its commanding position on Ludgate Hill dominating the skyline since 1710.

Continuing along the Thames, we made our way to London Bridge, the historic crossing that has connected the City with Southwark for nearly 2,000 years. The current bridge dates from 1973, but the location has been a vital river crossing since Roman times.

Evening Walk to Gymkhana

As evening approached and the winter twilight began to settle over London, we walked from London Bridge through the City toward Mayfair. The route took us through the atmospheric streets of the financial district, now quieting after the working day, and into the elegant West End neighborhoods.

This day-long walking tour covered several miles through central London, from the luxury of Knightsbridge through the royal and political heart of Westminster, across the Thames to the ancient City of London, and finally to Mayfair for our Michelin-starred dinner. It was an exhausting but exhilarating introduction to London - layers of history revealed at every turn, from Roman roads to Victorian engineering to modern architecture, all compressed into one of the world's most walkable capital cities.

London on Foot: Our walking route from Knightsbridge to Gymkhana covered approximately 5-6 miles through central London, passing through Knightsbridge, Westminster, the South Bank, and the City. This is the best way to experience London's incredible density of landmarks - comfortable shoes and a good map (or smartphone) are essential!

Gymkhana - Michelin Star Dining

To cap off our first day in London, we had reservations at Gymkhana, a prestigious Michelin two-star Indian restaurant in Mayfair. The restaurant takes its inspiration from the elite clubs of India during the British Raj era, creating an atmosphere that blends colonial elegance with contemporary sophistication.

The dining experience was extraordinary - creative Indian cuisine elevated to fine dining standards with exceptional presentation and complex flavors. Each dish showcased premium ingredients and masterful technique, from the tandoor-grilled meats to the rich curries and innovative small plates. The restaurant's intimate, club-like atmosphere with its dark wood paneling and vintage hunting trophies created the perfect ambiance for our celebration of arriving in the UK.

Our 9:00 PM reservation was perfectly timed, allowing us to enjoy a leisurely meal before taking the short walk to Green Park station. From there, we caught the Piccadilly Line back to Hatton Cross, completing our circuit of central London. Despite the long travel day and jet lag, the combination of London's historic sights, miles of walking, and world-class dining made for an unforgettable start to our United Kingdom adventure.

Dining at Gymkhana: This two-Michelin-star restaurant requires reservations well in advance, especially for weekend evenings. The tasting menu offers the best sampling of Chef Karam Sethi's creative Indian cuisine. Located in Mayfair, it's easily accessible via Piccadilly Circus or Green Park tube stations.
